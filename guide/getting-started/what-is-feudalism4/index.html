    What is Feudalism 4?¶

    Feudalism 4 (F4) is a medieval-fantasy roleplay game system for Second Life. Your character has stats, vitals, and skills; you earn experience, fight, hunt, gather, farm, and craft using objects placed around a region. Everything you do as a player runs through one wearable HUD.

    The core loop¶

    At its heart, Feudalism 4 is a loop you'll repeat in many flavors:

    graph LR
  A[Gather raw materials] --> B[Craft into goods]
  B --> C[Use, trade, or sell goods]
  C --> D[Earn XP and coin]
  D --> E[Raise stats and skills]
  E --> A
    • Gather — forage bushes, mine ore, chop wood, hunt animals, milk livestock, draw water.
    • Craft — turn raw materials into food, drink, tools, weapons, and trade goods at workstations.
    • Use & trade — eat food, equip gear, give items to other players, sell for coin.
    • Progress — activities award XP, which becomes Available Points you spend to raise your stats.

    What runs the game¶

    Piece What it is
    Players HUD A worn attachment that tracks your health, stamina, mana, and XP, runs combat, and talks to world objects.
    Setup HUD A web panel (opened from the HUD) where you create and edit your character — stats, class, inventory, and game mode.
    Worn tools Up to two storage pouches, plus optional drink skin and water bucket slotted on the HUD. Liquids and water live in personal inventory; skins and buckets open filtered views. See Personal Inventory.
    World objects Ovens, anvils, fields, beehives, gatherables, targets, chests, and more — the things you touch to play.
    The Meter An optional attachment that shows your name, title, and bars in floating text above your head.

    A roleplay system, not a story¶

    Feudalism 4 provides the mechanics — dice, stats, items, crafting, combat. It does not dictate your region's story, laws, or economy. Each universe (region or group of regions) sets its own rules and can:

    • enable or disable features (combat, mana, currency, specific stations);
    • filter which species, classes, and genders are available;
    • decide consent rules for combat, capture, death, and theft.

    When guides disagree, your universe wins

    If something here doesn't match what you see in-world, your universe has likely customized it. Ask local staff — they have the final word on rules.
