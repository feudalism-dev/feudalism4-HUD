    Game Modes & Resting¶

    Your HUD has a few modes that change how you're treated in play, plus a rest system for recovering health, stamina, and mana.

    Game modes¶

    Set your mode on the Setup HUD → Options tab (some HUD rest menus also switch it).

    Mode What it means for you
    Roleplay The default. Full stats, XP, combat, and crafting are active.
    Tournament PvP-balanced: combat stats are treated as 5 for everyone, with fixed display pools, so fights are about skill, not character power. The Meter always shows bars.
    OOC Out of character. The Meter shows an OOC marker. Leaving roleplay for OOC saves your pools and XP.
    AFK Away from keyboard. The Meter shows an AFK marker.
    None / ★ Hides the Meter text.

    Switching modes saves your data

    Leaving roleplay mode (for OOC, AFK, or tournament) triggers a save of your XP and pools — a good habit before you log off.

    Resting & recovery¶

    Open the rest menu with the Rest (heart) button on your HUD.

    In-character rest. Your character sits, and every 5 seconds you regain:

    • +1 Health
    • +1 Stamina
    • +1 Mana (if you have mana)

    Resting stops automatically when you're fully recovered, or when you choose Stop Resting (which also saves your data).

    Instantly refills health, stamina, and mana to maximum. It's an out-of-character convenience for between scenes — not an in-character healing effect.

    Ends a rest early. Do this before traveling or fighting so you're not stuck in the rest animation.

    Player steps — recover after a fight

    1. Get somewhere safe.
    2. Touch Rest (heart) → IC Rest.
    3. Wait for "You are fully recovered!", or tap Stop Resting.
    4. Between scenes (OOC), use OOC Reset instead.

    Passive stamina drain (optional)¶

    Some universes enable a slow stamina drain — for example −1 stamina every 10 minutes in roleplay or tournament, with optional extra drain while moving. It's off by default, and there's no drain in OOC, AFK, or ★ modes. Ask staff whether your region uses it.

    In-world rest objects (beds, campfires) may also restore vitals on their own timers — sit and follow their instructions.
